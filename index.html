Abstract

Electrolaryngeal (EL) speech, produced using an electrolarynx device by laryngectomees, often sounds unnatural due to its monotonous pitch and mechanical excitation. We propose an end-to-end deep learning-based model to enhance EL speech by reconstructing its intonation and transforming its voice into a natural one. Our method leverages deep learning-based any-to-many voice conversion (VC) models, which convert a speakerâ€™s voice to that of another while preserving the linguistic content. However, EL speech is out-of-distribution for existing VC models, and collecting large-scale EL speech datasets is challenging. To address this, we collected a small EL speech dataset and finetuned a pretrained any-to-many VC model to convert normal speech to EL speech, allowing us to augment a large-scale normal speech dataset with corresponding EL speech samples. These paired samples were then used to finetune the pretrained VC model for converting EL speech back to normal speech. Subjective evaluation confirmed the effectiveness of our approach, showing that the converted speech was significantly preferred over that produced by the pretrained VC model in terms of both intonation naturalness and intelligibility.
